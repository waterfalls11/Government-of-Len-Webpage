# Emergency Message Information

There are 3 levels to the Len Emergency Alerting System (LEAS). 

* Aware
This is like a watch. We are aware of a threat, but it is not yet imminent. Be aware and take action to reduce impacts of the threat.
* Alert
This means a threat is imminent, but not yet occurring. This is most often used during storms, where a storm is approaching, but not yet impacting Len. 
* ACT
This is like a warning. The threat is occurring. Take immediate action to protect yourself.

An alert may posses an EHA (extreme hazard awareness) tag if a person may be severely injured if they don’t heed the warning. The following criteria must also be met: 
* has a time to impact that is less than an hour,
* and a markedly large threat to life or property.

The alert categories are as follows:

Severe Storm

Tornado

Cyclone

Flood

Heat

Cold

Wildfire

Winter Weather/Winter Storm

Special Administrative Message (no category)
	Shelter in Place 
	Act of Violence
	Infrastructure (power, water, etc.) Outage
